⚠️ First, About That New Weight Training Method:

“Start with the highest weight, push as much as possible, then go to lower weights.”

That’s basically reverse pyramid training — a legit method for strength and hypertrophy, but only if done right.

✅ Use it like this:

  • Start with your heaviest set → ~4–6 reps
  • Only if warm-up sets are done first (never hit top weight cold)
  • Drop the weight ~10–15% per set
  • Increase reps as you drop weight → e.g., 6 → 8 → 10
  • Keep rest long between heavy sets (~90–120 sec)

This method works well for compound lifts, not so great for mind-muscle isolation (face pulls, curls, lateral raises).

🔧 Also: Never chase “as much as possible” if it kills form — especially on push/pull machines or shoulder work. That’s how asymmetries + neck pain creep back in.

🍽️ Strategic Eating Plan (No Whey, No Creatine, No Excuses)

You won’t have time for a full meal, but you must not skip this.

Option 1:

  • 1 glass milk (add powdered nuts or soaked dates for more calories)

Option 2:

  • 2 slices peanut butter toast + banana
  • 1 boiled egg or leftover sabzi

Why? You’re creating a caloric foundation. Even 300–400 kcal here makes a huge difference over 6 months.

🍱 College Lunch (~12:30–1:30 PM)

Make this your biggest controlled meal.

  • White rice + dal + eggs or chicken or paneer
  • Add ghee/curd to raise calories
  • Carry extra chapatis or boiled eggs if mess food is weak

🧃 Pre-Gym Bulking Shake (5:30–6:00 PM) — Homemade

✅ THIS is your best time for a shake.

Why here?

  • You’re low on energy before lifting
  • You’ll digest it during your gym commute
  • It fuels the session and primes recovery

🍌 Simple Whole-Food Bulking Shake:

  • 1 ripe banana
  • 300 ml whole milk or nut milk
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp soaked almonds or cashews
  • 1 tbsp jaggery or honey
  • (optional) oats or cooked rice to boost carbs

🌀 Blend. Chill if needed. Chug.

→ That’s 500–700 kcal with real food, no whey, no creatine, no bloat.

🍛 Post-Gym Dinner (~8:00–9:00 PM)

  • 1 paratha or ghee roti
  • Cooked veg + curd
  • Add peanuts, nuts, or pickle for extra fats

🌙 Before Bed (10:30–11:00 PM)

  • 1 glass milk
  • Handful of nuts or leftover paratha
  • Or a second mini meal (boiled eggs, curd rice, oats, etc.)
